## Design Context

### Users
Declarative is used primarily by parents, caregivers, and other supporters of neurodivergent children, especially families navigating PDA-related communication challenges. They are often using the product in emotionally loaded moments and need interfaces that reduce friction, feel safe, and make the next step obvious.

### Brand Personality
Calm, simple, trustworthy.

The product should feel supportive, grounded, and human. It should avoid flashy or overly "techy" visuals that compete with the emotional seriousness of the use case.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light, reassuring interface with soft color tints, clear hierarchy, and restrained decoration. Favor white or near-white surfaces, subtle tinted borders, gentle shadows, and a small number of warm/cool accents that connect with the rest of the site. Avoid dramatic gradients, dark hero treatments, or design flourishes that feel disconnected from the main app.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize calm over spectacle.
2. Keep layout and copy simple enough to understand at a glance.
3. Use visual emphasis sparingly and only to clarify important choices.
4. Match the established site pattern before inventing new UI language.
5. Make every support flow feel trustworthy, low-pressure, and easy to navigate.
